Facade

Oak, Computer Codes
2024

  Facade invites viewers to embrace technology through art, finding beauty and inspiration in the most unexpected places. This piece recreates an aquarium-like space, where an augmented reality garden eel leisurely rests at the bottom of a waterscape while a swarm of wood-carved fish dance above, crafting a visual narrative that blends the real and the virtual. By merging traditional craftsmanship with augmented reality, "Facade" highlights the endless possibilities of creative expression in the digital age.
  The augmented reality component was created using Blender and Aframe WebAR. To enhance user engagement, I set up the project with an instructional sign that provides clear, step-by-step guidance. This sign displays a QR code that links directly to the website hosting the augmented reality experience. Once the user scans the QR code with their smartphone, they are directed to the website where the augmented reality application is hosted. By aiming their camera at the marker positioned beneath the hanging wood-carved piece, the augmented reality content is triggered, allowing users to enjoy the augmented reality experience.
  Facade received an unexpectedly positive response from the audience during the art show. It emerged as one of the most interactive pieces I have presented, fostering a deeper connection with the viewers and enhancing overall engagement.
