Flap Flap Revolution

2018

Flap Flap Revolution was created at SBUHack 2018. It is a sound-controlled gameplay, combining elements from Flappy Bird and Dance Dance Revolution. The gameplay mechanics involve the user's voice input to control the movements of the Flappy Bird avatar with the primary objective of maneuvering the bird to capture stars, which are synchronized with the musical beats, akin to the arrows in Dance Dance Revolution.
As the team lead, I orchestrated the project's development by three core tasks. Two of our computer science teammates were assigned to the intricate task of mapping stars to the music, ensuring a seamless integration of gameplay elements.
Simultaneously, I focused on crafting the voice control script, a pivotal component in achieving the project's distinct interaction mechanism. Collaboratively, we worked on preparing the necessary game assets to ensure a cohesive and visually engaging user experience. In the end, I integrated all the components in Unity to get the final result.
Our team won the Best Game Dev prize. It was fun watching people scream when they played our game during the presentation. :3
